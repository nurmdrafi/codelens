## Commit strategy

24 commits, version bump last. Group on `dev` branch, merge to `main` as a single PR before tagging.

**Part A (docs)**: Steps 1–5
**Part B (diff bug)**: Step 6
**Part C (manifest + npx)**: Steps 7–9
**Part D (template)**: Step 10
**Part E (token efficiency)**: Steps 11–14 + 13.5
**Part F (polish)**: Steps 15–17
**Part G (stack-aware doctor)**: Step 18
**Part H (custom-checks)**: Steps 19–20
**Part I (languages)**: Steps 21–23
**Version bump**: Step 24 (last)

**Sequencing rationale**:
- Part E lands after Part A so CLAUDE.md's line-count claim (Step 2) can be re-updated in Step 13.5 after the refactor shrinks the agent.
- Part I (languages) lands after Part H (custom-checks) because both share the Phase 0.5 config-load pattern — H establishes it, I reuses it.
- Part G (doctor stack detection) technically depends on Part I (`languages.json` exists) — but Step 18 can ship with a hardcoded manifest map and Step 23 makes it config-driven. If Part I slips, Part G still works.
- Version bump is last so the CHANGELOG accurately lists everything that shipped.

Each part is a PR-sized chunk that can land independently. If any part slips, the rest still ship value.
